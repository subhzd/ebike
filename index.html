E-Bike or Motorcycle?
Know the California Laws.

Many popular "e-bikes" marketed to teens are actually illegal to ride without a license. Understand the 3 e-bike classes, new 2026 safety rules, and local Santa Clara County regulations.

The 3 Legal E-Bike Classes

In California, an e-bike MUST fall into one of these three categories to be allowed on bike paths and roads without a license.

Class 1

Pedal Assist
  • Max Speed: 20 mph
  • Throttle: No
  • Age limit: None

Helmet: Required if under 18.

Access: Allowed anywhere regular bicycles are allowed.

Class 2

Throttle
  • Max Speed: 20 mph
  • Throttle: Yes
  • Age limit: None

Helmet: Required if under 18.

Access: Generally allowed on bike paths and lanes.

Class 3

Speed Pedal-Assist
  • Max Speed: 28 mph
  • Throttle: No
  • Age limit: 16+ Only

Helmet: Required for ALL ages.

Access: Prohibited on most off-street trails. Road lanes mostly.

When is it legally a Motorcycle?

If your child's "e-bike" (like a Sur-Ron, Talaria, or high-powered Super73) does any of the following, it is legally classified as a Motor-Driven Cycle or Moped by the DMV:

  • The motor is more than 750 Watts.
  • It has a throttle that propels it past 20 mph.
  • It assists past 28 mph.
  • It does not have fully operable pedals.
Consequences: Riding these on sidewalks, bike lanes, or trails is illegal. Operating them requires a valid M1 or M2 motorcycle license, DMV registration, and a DOT-approved motorcycle helmet. Kids under 16 caught riding these can have the vehicle impounded.

New 2026 California Laws

Battery Certification

To prevent fires, all e-bikes and batteries sold in CA must now meet UL 2849 safety standards. "Mystery batteries" are illegal to sell.

Day & Night Reflectors

A rear red reflector or a red rear light with a built-in reflector is now required at ALL times—both day and night.

No Illegal Mods

Using apps or "tuning" devices to unlock speeds past 28mph instantly turns the e-bike into an unregistered motor vehicle.

Minor Violations

Minors who receive a helmet violation while riding an e-bike must now complete a CHP-developed online safety training program.

Santa Clara County Park Rules

Local jurisdictions can restrict where e-bikes are allowed. Santa Clara County Parks have specific rules to keep trails safe for hikers, horses, and cyclists.

Where you CAN Ride

  • Class 1 and Class 2 e-bikes are allowed on all paved and dirt "Class 1 bikeways" in County Parks.

Where you CANNOT Ride

  • Class 3 e-bikes are strictly PROHIBITED on County Park trails.
  • Fully motorized/battery-operated non-pedal devices are prohibited.

Strict Helmet Rule: All persons, regardless of age, MUST wear an ANSI/Snell approved helmet on unpaved trails in Santa Clara County Parks.
